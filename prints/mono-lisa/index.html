Mono Lisa

In the quiet language of shadow, she becomes a portrait of restraint, not posed, but remembered.

A face softened by silence. A gaze turned inward. Light resting gently across skin, hair, and breath, as if the frame itself paused to listen.

She is a secret held in black and white.

A study of elegance, stillness, and the kind of beauty that does not ask to be seen — it simply remains.
